Press Release:

C. R. Ettinger Gallery is pleased to present Temples, an exhibition of prints by Ryan Parker.

In 2017-18 on a Fulbright fellowship to India, Parker explored connections between ancient temple architecture and traditional Indian block printing. The early Indian architects, particularly in temples of the Dravidian style, used a repetition of form, pattern, and image in their designs, building architecture that embodies ideas of cosmic birth and regeneration. Intrinsic to the printmaking medium is the idea of multiples, which are created from a matrix (a woodblock, a copper plate, a lithographic stone) to produce an infinite number of printed works.

This connection, along with his own subsequent studies in transcendental meditation and computer programming, led to these images of invented temple maps.

About the Artist:

Ryan Parker received his MFA in printmaking from Tyler School of Art, Temple University in Rome, Italy and Philadelphia. 

He has participated in artist residencies at Frans Masereel Centrum (Belgium), Cork Printmakers (Ireland), and Ballinglen (Ireland). Ryan was also a 2017–18 Fulbright-Nehru Fellow in India with research on ancient architecture and India’s fabric arts, and in 2018-19, he was the Grant Wood Fellow in Printmaking at The University of Iowa.
